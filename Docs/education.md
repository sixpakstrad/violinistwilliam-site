## Teaching Philosophy and Methodology

The secondary education content describes Winspiration Studio as a name derived from `win` and `inspiration`.

Core ideas:

- William's own work untangling violin technique shaped his teaching.
- His desire to improve, win auditions, and become a reliable violinist informs the studio approach.
- William is a Suzuki Method registered violin instructor.
- He also uses other methods when they better fit a student's learning pattern.
- Lessons emphasize patience, control, focus, and planning.
- Quality practice is framed as more important than quantity alone.
- Music is treated as a language learned through listening, speaking, and eventually reading.

For children:

- William works with parents to build a musical household.
- Families are encouraged to create regular practice time.
- Early learners may learn first pieces by memory before they are strong readers.
- Pieces are reinforced through listening, repetition, and gradual skill-building.

For adults:

- Lessons focus on refining technique and skill.
- The page welcomes students solving persistent technical problems or returning to playing.
- Musicality remains central at every level.

## Pricing

Current title:

`In-person or virtual lesson options.`

Location and format notes:

- William teaches privately out of St. Paul, St. Louis Park, and St. Anthony.
- Virtual lessons are available for students who want to learn from home.

Rates:
