## Bow Rehair and Repair

Section title:

`Control, response, and a bow that feels reliable in the hand.`

The page explains that a well-maintained bow improves control, articulation, and consistency. It notes that worn hair, damaged fittings, a loose button, poor camber, or structural damage can affect both feel and sound.

Bow services include:

- Bow rehairs
- Cleaning and refinishing
- Thumb grip and winding replacement
- Tip replacement
- Metal tip replacement
- Frog crack repair and missing wood fill
- Straightening and camber adjustment
- Button and screw repairs
- Pearl replacement
- Broken head repair
- Broken stick repair

## Setup and Maintenance

Section title:

`Small adjustments can make an instrument easier to play.`

The page explains that bridge fit, soundpost placement, peg function, fingerboard condition, string height, and fittings all influence comfort, tone, and playability.

Instrument services include:

- Initial setup
- Custom setup and playability adjustment
- Tone adjustment
- Edge gluing
- Basic crack repair
- Fingerboard planing
- Peg refitting
- New bridge or bridge refit
- New soundpost or soundpost refit
- Filling worn edges
- Tailpiece replacement
- Chin rest replacement

## Training and Background

Section title:

`Bench work informed by the realities of playing.`

William's repair and setup work is described as grounded in formal training with Jennifer Becker and continued hands-on study with makers and bow specialists including Rodney Mohr, Roger Zabinski, and David Orlin.

The page connects this repair background to William's performance and teaching experience, especially his ability to understand what players feel under the hand and bow.
